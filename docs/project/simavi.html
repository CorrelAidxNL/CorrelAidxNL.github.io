Simavi

Analyzed baseline and midline surveys that measure the impact of Simavi's WASH programme in three different countries, specifically focusing on Gender Equality and Social Inclusion.

Founded in 1925, Simavi encourages girls and women to claim their right to equality and basic needs, resulting in a physically, mentally, and socially healthier society for everyone. Their work is women-centred, rights-based, and builds lasting change.

They start and facilitate projects over two broad programmes: water, sanitation and hygiene (WASH) and sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR). This project’s goal was to analyze baseline and midline surveys that measure the impact of their WASH programme in 3 different countries, specifically focusing on Gender Equality and Social Inclusion.
